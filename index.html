OPTIMIZATION OF WIND TURBINE CONFIGURATIONS FOR EFFICIENT EXPRESSWAY POWER HARVESTING | GROUP

Team EcoMech | University of Peradeniya
Supervised by Prof. Prasanna Gunawardane
(Apr. 2024 – Jun. 2024)

This project explored the potential of harvesting renewable energy from expressways by integrating vertical axis wind turbines (VAWTs) along high-traffic highways. The aim was to design and optimize turbine configurations that could efficiently harness wind generated by both natural flows and passing vehicles, offering a sustainable alternative to fossil-fuel-based power generation.
Objectives:
  • Analyze wind flow patterns near expressways, influenced by traffic and natural conditions.
  • Design and simulate efficient Darrieus, Savonius, and hybrid (Darrieus-Savonius) turbine models.
  • Optimize turbine configurations using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) in ANSYS Fluent.
  • Compare torque, tip speed ratio (TSR), and power coefficient (Cp) to identify the most effective design.
  • Assess practical feasibility, environmental impact, and future scalability of roadside turbine installations.
Methodology:
  • Simulation Tools: SolidWorks (CAD), ANSYS Fluent (CFD analysis).
  • Boundary Conditions: Simulated at highway wind speed of 6 m/s.
  • Airfoils Tested: NACA 0012 and NACA 0018 profiles for Darrieus turbines.
  • Configurations: Darrieus VAWT, Savonius (2-blade & 3-blade), Hybrid (2-blade & 3-blade).
  • Key Metrics: Torque, power coefficient (Cp), and self-starting ability.
Key Findings:
  • Hybrid VAWTs offered the best balance between self-starting capability and efficiency.
  • 3-Blade Hybrid Turbine: Highest torque at low TSRs, suitable for variable winds.
  • Darrieus Turbine: Achieved highest Cp (~0.40) but needed external assistance to start.
  • Savonius Turbines: Good self-starting but less efficient at high speeds.
  • Airfoil: NACA 0012 performed better than NACA 0018 with higher efficiency and delayed stall.
Outcomes & Contributions:
  • Proposed a novel hybrid VAWT design optimized for expressway energy harvesting.
  • Developed a CFD-based framework for turbine evaluation.
  • Showcased scalability for powering streetlights, traffic sensors, and decentralized energy systems.
Future Scope:
  • Prototyping and real-world field testing of optimized designs.
  • Feasibility studies on economics and environmental benefits.
  • Integration with smart highway infrastructure.
Technologies & Tools:

SolidWorks, ANSYS Fluent (CFD), NACA Airfoils, MATLAB (Data Analysis).

SHELL & TUBE HEAT EXCHANGER – DESIGN, MODELING & ANALYSIS | GROUP

Team Project | University of Peradeniya
(Jun. 2024 – Jul. 2024)

This project focused on the design, modeling, and performance analysis of a shell and tube heat exchanger, one of the most widely used heat transfer devices in industrial applications. The objective was to compare parallel flow vs. counter flow configurations and evaluate the effectiveness of two different baffle designs: Cut Segment and Doughnut–Disk.
Objectives:
  • Design a shell and tube heat exchanger with realistic industrial parameters.
  • Perform material selection for shell and tube construction.
  • Develop fluid domains and generate meshes for CFD simulations.
  • Analyze temperature distribution, pressure drop, and flow vectors under different baffle and flow configurations.
  • Compare thermal effectiveness across designs and recommend the best configuration.
Tools & Methods:
  • Design & Modeling: SolidWorks, ANSYS Design Modeler, ANSYS SpaceClaim
  • Simulation: ANSYS Fluent (temperature, velocity, pressure contours)
  • Materials: Stainless steel, copper, carbon steel (optimized in software)
  • Performance Criteria: Shell outlet & tube outlet temperatures, turbulence, and heat transfer effectiveness
Key Findings:
  • Counter flow significantly outperformed parallel flow in heat transfer efficiency
  • Doughnut–Disk baffle with counter flow was the most effective design, reducing inlet air temp by ~100°C
  • Cut Segment baffles performed better in parallel flow due to higher water volume near tube inlets
  • Mesh limitations in ANSYS Student Version required adaptations, but trends showed counter flow superiority
Outcomes & Contributions:
  • Produced a complete design-to-simulation pipeline for shell & tube heat exchangers
  • Delivered comparative analysis of two baffle designs & two flow patterns
  • Recommended the Doughnut–Disk counter flow heat exchanger as the optimal industrial design

HOUSE PRICE PREDICTION USING NEURAL NETWORKS | GROUP

Team Fuzzy Wizards | University of Peradeniya
(Apr. 2024 – Jun. 2024)

This project focused on building a Neural Network-based prediction system to estimate house prices in Bengaluru. The model was trained on real estate data with features such as location, square footage, number of bedrooms, bathrooms, and amenities, aiming to outperform traditional regression-based methods.
Objectives:
  • Develop a robust Artificial Neural Network (ANN) for house price prediction.
  • Preprocess data by handling missing values, encoding categorical variables, and normalizing features.
  • Train and validate the model using different configurations and hyperparameters.
  • Compare performance with traditional ML models (Random Forest, XGBoost).
  • Evaluate results using MAE, RMSE, and R² score.
Tools & Methods:
  • Dataset: Bengaluru housing dataset (Kaggle)
  • Model: 4-layer MLP (64 input neurons, 2 hidden layers × 32 neurons ReLU, 1 output neuron)
  • Training: 80/20 split, 100 epochs with early stopping, batch size 32
  • Optimizer: Adam | Loss Function: MSE
  • Evaluation: MAE, RMSE, R² score
Key Findings:
  • ANN achieved MAE: 0.28, RMSE: 0.23, and R²: 0.24.
  • Outperformed Random Forest and XGBoost in capturing nonlinear relationships.
  • Predicted prices tracked closely with actual values in test data.
Outcomes & Contributions:
  • Developed a complete end-to-end ANN pipeline for house price prediction.
  • Validated model superiority over traditional ML methods in accuracy and scalability.
  • Proposed deployment as a real-time prediction tool for buyers and investors.
  • Suggested future improvements: larger datasets, Sri Lanka-specific data, and deeper architectures.
