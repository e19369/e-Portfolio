OPTIMIZATION OF WIND TURBINE CONFIGURATIONS FOR EFFICIENT EXPRESSWAY POWER HARVESTING | GROUP

Team EcoMech | University of Peradeniya
Guided by Prof. Prasanna Gunawardane

(Apr. 2024‚Äì Jun. 2024)

This project explored the potential of harvesting renewable energy from expressways by integrating vertical axis wind turbines (VAWTs) along high-traffic highways. The aim was to design and optimize turbine configurations that could efficiently harness wind generated by both natural flows and passing vehicles, offering a sustainable alternative to fossil-fuel-based power generation.
Objectives:
  • Analyze wind flow patterns near expressways, influenced by traffic and natural conditions.
  • Design and simulate efficient Darrieus, Savonius, and hybrid (Darrieus-Savonius) turbine models.
  • Optimize turbine configurations using Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) in ANSYS Fluent.
  • Compare torque, tip speed ratio (TSR), and power coefficient (Cp) to identify the most effective design.
  • Assess practical feasibility, environmental impact, and future scalability of roadside turbine installations.
Methodology:
  • Simulation Tools: SolidWorks (CAD), ANSYS Fluent (CFD analysis).
  • Boundary Conditions: Simulated at highway wind speed of 6 m/s.
  • Airfoils Tested: NACA 0012 and NACA 0018 profiles for Darrieus turbines.
  • Configurations: Darrieus VAWT, Savonius (2-blade & 3-blade), Hybrid (2-blade & 3-blade).
  • Key Metrics: Torque, power coefficient (Cp), and self-starting ability.
Key Findings:
  • Hybrid VAWTs offered the best balance between self-starting capability and efficiency.
  • 3-Blade Hybrid Turbine: Highest torque at low TSRs, suitable for variable winds.
  • Darrieus Turbine: Achieved highest Cp (~0.40) but needed external assistance to start.
  • Savonius Turbines: Good self-starting but less efficient at high speeds.
  • Airfoil: NACA 0012 performed better than NACA 0018 with higher efficiency and delayed stall.
Outcomes & Contributions:
  • Proposed a novel hybrid VAWT design optimized for expressway energy harvesting.
  • Developed a CFD-based framework for turbine evaluation.
  • Showcased scalability for powering streetlights, traffic sensors, and decentralized energy systems.
Future Scope:
  • Prototyping and real-world field testing of optimized designs.
  • Feasibility studies on economics and environmental benefits.
  • Integration with smart highway infrastructure.
Technologies & Tools:

SolidWorks, ANSYS Fluent (CFD), NACA Airfoils, MATLAB (Data Analysis).
